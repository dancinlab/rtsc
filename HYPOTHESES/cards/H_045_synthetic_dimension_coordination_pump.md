## Hypothesis (frozen pre-register)

Add an internal **"synthetic" hopping axis** — an extra index `m = 0..N-1` with uniform synthetic hopping `t_s`, threaded as Josephson neighbours **without adding any real space** — onto a 2D flat band. The claim is that the extra coordination on this synthetic axis *decouples flat-band pairing from stiffness-coordination* and **pumps up the physical phase stiffness** D_s that sets T_BKT:

```
D_s(N) ~ D_s(1) * (1 + c*(N-1)),  c > 0,   real-space <g> unchanged
T_BKT  = (pi/2) * D_s                        (Emery-Kivelson freeze relation)
```

so that for `N >= 3` the real T_BKT clears the 134–164 K spin-fluctuation / phase-stiffness ambient ceiling. The bet: the quantum-metric no-go that caps a single real-space flat band "may or may not bind on a synthetic axis."

## Which of the freeze premises this violates

The freeze ceiling was measured on hosts that are {Q=0 / single-particle-flat / **crystalline (real-space coordination)** / quasiparticle-coherent / equilibrium}. This escape attempts to violate the **real-space crystalline coordination** premise by routing the extra hopping through an *internal index*. The crux — genuinely open before the run — is whether an internal-index hopping contributes to the **physical** Kubo superfluid weight or only to an internal-index susceptibility.

## Why (mechanism + literature grounding)

The phase stiffness that bounds T_BKT is the **physical Kubo superfluid weight** — the long-wavelength current–current response to a **real** electromagnetic vector potential `A_x`. The current operator is generated by the **Peierls phase** `exp[i (e/ℏ) A·r_ij]`, where `r_ij` is the **real-space displacement** of bond (i,j) (Scalapino–White–Zhang PRB 47 7995, 1993). A synthetic-dimension bond connects `m -> m+1` at the **same real-space site**, so `r_ij = 0`: a real vector potential imprints **zero** Peierls phase on it. The synthetic hopping `t_s` enters the real-direction current operator with weight `(displacement)^2 = 0` and contributes **nothing** to D_xx / D_yy. It feeds only a **synthetic-index susceptibility** D_ss — the response to a *fictitious synthetic gauge field* (Boada et al. PRL 108 133001, 2012; Celi et al. PRL 112 043001, 2014) that no real vortex couples to. Equivalently, in `D_s^{μ,ν} = (|U|/ℏ²) ν(1-ν) <g_{μ,ν}>` (Peotta–Törmä Nat. Commun. 6 8944, 2015; Liang et al. PRB 95 024515, 2017), `g_{μ,ν}` is built from `∂(Bloch eigvec)/∂k_μ`: for μ,ν ∈ {x,y} the **real** momentum, so `<g_xx>(N)` is **N-independent**; only `<g_ss>` grows. Multilayer/internal stacking does not pump the *physical* superfluid weight per area (arXiv:2111.00807).

T_BKT = (π/2) D_s. Escape requires real T_BKT(N) to grow and exceed 164 K; the honest-null is `D_s_real(N) = D_s_real(1)`.
