## VERDICT: 🟠 OPEN / NOVEL (genuinely unrealized, plausible, actively pursued — but the GLUE LEVER is a graveyard)

The named trio has never been built or co-measured, and — the load-bearing finding — **proximity SC
induced in a metal by an adjacent excitonic insulator / its exciton condensate has NEVER been
measured. It is entirely theoretical (Allender–Bray–Bardeen-class, 1973 → 2026, still unrealized).**
The architecture is therefore a real first if built, but the specific *excitonic-glue* lever is the
weakest part — its 50-year experimental score is zero. The closest REAL measured precedents are
(a) emergent SC at a *kagome-magnet/normal-metal* interface (TbMn6Sn6/Au, Tc≈3.6 K — but the glue is a
ferromagnet, not an excitonic insulator) and (b) Ta2NiSe5's *own* pressure-SC, which is a NEGATIVE for
the excitonic route (see Q2/Q4). This is not 🟢 (no matching device exists) and not flatly 🔴 (no no-go
theorem; active theory frontier), but the excitonic-glue sub-lever has a graveyard history that should
temper any fab spend.

## Q1 — Has this architecture (or a close analog) already been BUILT and MEASURED?

**No matching device. The closest REAL measured analog is an emergent-SC kagome-magnet/metal interface
— but the "glue" is a ferromagnet, not an excitonic insulator.**

- **Closest real precedent (measured, 🟢 for the *geometry-metal-interface* half only):**
  *Emergent superconductivity in topological-kagome-magnet/metal heterostructures*, Nat. Commun. (2023),
  PMC10622413. The kagome magnet **TbMn6Sn6** (itself NON-superconducting) capped with a normal metal
  (Au / Ag / Ni) develops an interface SC: resistance drop to ~60% of R(6K) at **Tc ≈ 3.6 K** (Au-capped
  (001)); point-contact Tc ≈ 3.4 K. Proposed mechanism: interface spin-triplet (p-wave), TRS-breaking,
  possibly chiral topological SC, coupled to the kagome magnetic order. **This is the strongest "found"
  result for the campaign's metal-interface idea — but the inducing layer is a magnet, not an EI.**
- **Kagome-metal proximity SC (conventional direction):** *Anisotropic proximity-induced SC and edge
  supercurrent in kagome metal K1−xV3Sb5*, Sci. Adv. (2023) / arXiv:2012.05898 — here the kagome metal is
  the WEAK-LINK and a conventional SC supplies the pairs (opposite of the +@ direction). Real, measured.
- **No** flat-band-metal / hBN-spacer / excitonic-insulator trilayer with a transport SC result was found
  anywhere. The +@ direction (non-SC excitonic glue → induces SC in a flat-band metal) is unrealized.

## Q2 — Proximity-induced SC from an excitonic insulator specifically — ever measured?

**NEVER MEASURED. Purely theoretical.** Every result is a proposal / Ginzburg-Landau or Hartree-Fock
model; none is a device with measured Tc.

- *Superconductivity Induced by Strong Electron-Exciton Coupling in Doped Atomically Thin Semiconductor
  Heterostructures*, PRL **133**, 226903 (2024) — **theory proposal** (doped TMD heterostructures);
  "possible experimental realization … discussed," not realized.
- *Emergence of a condensate with finite-energy Cooper pairing in hybrid exciton/superconductor systems*,
  arXiv:2402.02747 (2024) — **theory** (time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau, Higgs spectra); realization only
  "discussed in heterostructures with TMD layers and semiconducting quantum wells."
- *Exciton-Enhanced Superconductivity in Monolayer Films of Aluminum*, arXiv:2409.12201 (2024) —
  **theory**; *proposes* the exciton mechanism as a post-hoc explanation of an Al-film enhancement; no
  measured excitonic Tc. (Al monolayer enhancement is real but its excitonic origin is unproven.)
- **Direct answer: there is no measured case of SC induced in a metal by an adjacent excitonic insulator
  or its exciton condensate.** The Allender–Bray–Bardeen excitonic mechanism remains, after ~50 years,
  experimentally unrealized.

## Q3 — The interlayer-plasmon / interlayer-exciton SC frontier — realized or still proposals?

**All still PROPOSALS. None experimentally realized.** Closest real measured result is an interlayer
exciton *condensate* (Coulomb-drag), but with NO superconductivity.

- **arXiv:2508.06195** — *Enhancing Plasmonic Superconductivity in Layered Materials via Dynamical
  Coulomb Engineering* — **theory**; the authors explicitly call suspended vdW bilayers a platform to
  realize "this long anticipated theoretical concept" → not yet realized.
- **arXiv:2410.09148** — *Unconventional SC mediated by exciton density wave fluctuations* — **theory**
  (self-consistent Hartree-Fock, charge-imbalanced bilayer semiconductors); "experimental signatures …
  discussed," no device.
- **arXiv:2508.09098** — *Interlayer exciton condensates between second Landau level orbitals in double
  bilayer graphene* — **THIS ONE IS A REAL MEASUREMENT**, but of an interlayer **exciton condensate**
  (Coulomb-drag in the quantum-Hall regime, 2.5 nm hBN spacer), **not superconductivity**. It is the
  closest real device to the +@ spacer geometry (bilayer / thin-hBN / interlayer condensate) but produces
  a drag-measured condensate, not a SC transition. Also relevant: arXiv:2601.07729 (2026, *theory*,
  predicted SC in mass-asymmetric electron-hole bilayers) — still a prediction.

## Q4 — Near-miss / negative results; modern status of the excitonic-SC graveyard

**The graveyard is NOT revived into a realized SC; it remains an active *theory* frontier with zero
device wins. And the lead glue's own SC is a NEGATIVE for the excitonic route.**

- **Ta2NiSe5 (the lead glue) pressure-SC is a near-miss NEGATIVE:** *Hybridization-gap formation and SC
  in the pressure-induced semimetallic phase of the EI Ta2NiSe5*, arXiv:2106.04396; optical-conductivity
  suppression arXiv:2208.12077. Ta2NiSe5 *does* superconduct (**Tc ≈ 1.2 K at ~8 GPa**) — **but only
  AFTER the excitonic-insulator state is destroyed by pressure** (gap fills, EI → semimetal at ~3 GPa),
  and the SC is attributed to **soft-phonon / electron-lattice coupling, not the excitonic instability.**
  i.e. when Ta2NiSe5 actually superconducts, the exciton condensate is GONE and phonons do the work — the
  opposite of using the live exciton as glue. This is the single most important negative for the lead
  candidate's premise.
- **Allender–Bray–Bardeen (1973) / Ginzburg / Little graveyard:** the excitonic mechanism has been
  re-proposed continuously (FeSe/SrTiO3 sandwich invoked as a possible host, doped-band-insulator
  spin-triplet PNAS 2019, exciton-polariton-mediated SC) but never produces a *measured* excitonic Tc.
  The 2024–2025 "new era of EI" reviews (JPSJ 94, 012001 (2025); J. Phys. Condens. Matter 36 review,
  10.1088/1361-648X/ad81a3) report that **excitonic INSULATORS** are now experimentally credible — but
  none reports **excitonic SUPERCONDUCTIVITY**. Insulator ≠ superconductor: the EI side advanced, the SC
  payoff did not.
- No *published negative* of the exact flat-band-metal + excitonic-glue trilayer was found (because no one
  has built it) — the negatives are the structural one above (Ta2NiSe5) and the 50-year null result.

## Q5 — CoSn / Ta2NiSe5 specifically — stacked / gated / doped / proximity-coupled toward SC?

- **CoSn:** characterized as a flat-band kagome metal (orbital-selective Dirac fermions, topological flat
  band, anomalous transport, measured QGT ∫tr g ≈ 2.87 / arXiv:2412.17809 cited in the synthesis paper) —
  **no stacking/gating/proximity-toward-SC paper found.** CoSn itself is not reported superconducting.
- **Ta2NiSe5:** EI characterization is extensive (Nat. Commun. 2021 s41467-021-22133-z direct excitonic
  instability; Frontiers 2025 RIXS review) and there is a Ta2NiSe5/WS2 vdW heterostructure in the
  literature — but **no Ta2NiSe5 heterostructure with a measured proximity-SC result.** The only Ta2NiSe5
  SC is the pressure-SC above, where the EI is destroyed.
- **CoSn + Ta2NiSe5 together: zero papers.** Jointly unrealized, confirming the campaign's claim.

## Research-first recommendation (one paragraph)

The literature does **not** already deliver the +@ result (so it is not a wasted 🟢), but it also does
**not** justify spending on the *excitonic-glue* fab path yet. The decisive finding is asymmetric: the
metal-interface half of the idea is real and measured (kagome-magnet/metal emergent SC, TbMn6Sn6/Au,
Tc≈3.6 K — PMC10622413), and interlayer *condensates* through thin hBN are real and measured
(arXiv:2508.09098) — but the *specific* lever the campaign depends on, **superconductivity proximity-
induced in a metal by an excitonic insulator, has NEVER been measured in 50 years** and every 2024–2026
instance (PRL 133.226903; arXiv:2402.02747, 2410.09148, 2508.06195, 2409.12201) is theory. Worse, the
lead glue Ta2NiSe5 only superconducts (1.2 K, 8 GPa) once its exciton condensate is *destroyed*, by
phonons — a direct structural negative for the premise. **Recommendation:** do NOT green-light synthesis
of the CoSn/hBN/Ta2NiSe5 excitonic-glue trilayer as the next spend. The cheaper, literature-justified
de-risking step is to first demonstrate the *missing physical primitive in isolation* — proximity SC from
an EI in ANY measured system — or to pivot the glue lever to a boson family with a measured proximity-SC
track record (the kagome-magnet/metal interface SC is the only real one, but it is a magnet, not a
≥349 meV clean q=0 EI). The +@ architecture stays 🟠 (sound, unrealized, no no-go), but the excitonic-glue
sub-lever should be treated as 🔴-leaning until a single excitonic proximity-SC measurement exists
anywhere. `absorbed=false` / GATE_OPEN unchanged. No material is claimed to BE an RTSC.
