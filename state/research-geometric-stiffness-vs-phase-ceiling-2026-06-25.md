## Q1 — Is D_s^geom LARGER or SMALLER than the cuprate phase stiffness? (Kelvin-equivalent)

### The formula and the normalization trap

The Peotta-Törmä geometric superfluid weight of an isolated flat band [VERIFIED, Peotta & Törmä, Nat.Commun. 6, 8944 (2015) / arXiv:1506.02815; as written in the campaign's own closed-negative synthesis paper, `state/papers/flatband-geometry-ambient-roomt-closed/main.tex`]:

```
D_s = 4 |U| ν(1−ν) ⟨g⟩            (Eq. peotta)
```

- |U| = attractive interaction, ν = band filling (maximal at ν=1/2), ⟨g⟩ = BZ-average of the Fubini-Study quantum metric.
- 2D BKT: k_B T_c^BKT ≤ (π/2) D_s [VERIFIED, standard BKT; synthesis paper Eq.].

**The normalization trap (load-bearing, adversarial).** The campaign's measured/computed "∫tr g ≈ 2.856" (H_024 CoSn TB fit) and the measured QGT g=2.87 (Kang arXiv:2412.17809) are the **QGT-convention metric INTEGRAL** I = (1/2π)∫_BZ tr g d²k — a dimensionless topological-class number (≥ |C|, the Chern bound). This is **NOT** the per-BZ average ⟨g⟩ that enters D_s in Eq. peotta. The synthesis paper states the realizable ⟨g⟩ entering the stiffness is **⟨g⟩ ~ 0.1-0.5** [VERIFIED, closed-negative paper §method: "D_geom = 4|U|ν(1−ν)⟨g⟩ with ⟨g⟩ ~ 0.1-0.5; the only place ⟨g⟩ diverges is at the band touching, cut off by Δ"]. **Plugging I≈2.86 into Eq. peotta as if it were ⟨g⟩ inflates D_s by ~6-30×** — that is exactly the harness optimism the L1/L2 limits flag. The honest ⟨g⟩ is O(0.1-0.5).

The geometric D_s is **~20-90× BELOW** the cuprate phase stiffness (134/1.5 ≈ 90× for MATBG; 134/8 ≈ 17× for Re6Se8Cl2). And vs the ~300 meV spin-fluctuation **pairing** scale, the geometric stiffness (~0.06-0.44 meV) is **~700-5000× below** — the pairing energy is nowhere near the bottleneck. The "70× enhancement" the synthesis-paper ED found [VERIFIED, route-0 green check] is 70× of the flat-band CONVENTIONAL stiffness (≈0, since the band velocity vanishes) — a real and correct statement that nonetheless leaves the ABSOLUTE stiffness tiny.

## Q2 — Is a flat-band geometric SC PAIRING-limited or STIFFNESS-limited?

**STIFFNESS-limited, unambiguously, and confirmed by direct measurement.** Tc = min(pairing scale, phase stiffness) [VERIFIED WebSearch, Uemura/Emery-Kivelson framing: "Tc is determined by the smaller of the pairing scale and the phase stiffness"]. For the flat-band geometric SC:

- pairing scale (if a 300 meV spin-fluctuation or a strong |U| glue): ~10²-10³ K coordinate — large.
- geometric phase stiffness: D_s^eff ~ 0.06-0.44 meV → ~1-8 K — **tiny, and far the smaller of the two.**

So Tc is pinned by the STIFFNESS, and a real flat-band SC is **stiffness-limited**. This is not inference — it is the **measured** statement: twisted trilayer graphene's transition is "controlled by phase fluctuations rather than Cooper-pair breaking" [VERIFIED, Banerjee Nature s41586-024-08444-3], i.e. the gap stays open above Tc (pairing is NOT the bottleneck) and phase stiffness sets Tc. MATBG likewise shows superfluid stiffness ~10× above the conventional band value yet Tc only ~1 K [VERIFIED, Tian Nature 2024/2025] — the geometric enhancement is real but the absolute stiffness ceiling still caps Tc near 1 K.

**Consequence for the dispatch's branch logic:** the dispatch posed "if D_s^geom ≥ pairing → pairing-limited → escape the cuprate ceiling to the ~252 K clean-pairing coordinate." The measured reality is the OTHER branch: **D_s^geom ≪ pairing → STIFFNESS-limited → a cuprate-like (worse) ceiling bites.** The flat-band route does NOT graduate to the clean-pairing 252 K coordinate; it is held ~20-90× *below* even the cuprate 134 K stiffness scale.

## Q3 — Counter-lenses (adversarial; none rescue the geometric route)

### 3a — Does the geometric D_s have its OWN ceiling? YES — the U·g saturation wall.

The Peotta-Törmä D_s ∝ |U|·⟨g⟩ is LINEAR in U **only in the isolated-flat-band regime |U| < band gap.** Two coupled bounds cap it [VERIFIED WebSearch, multiple]:

1. **Isolated-band condition:** D_s ∝ |U|⟨g⟩ requires |U| ≪ band gap; "when the band gap shrinks, the largest |U| for which the quantum metric is proportional to the superfluid weight decreases accordingly" [VERIFIED WebSearch, Lieb-lattice Commun.Phys. s42005-025-01964-y / arXiv:2404.12993]. **A LARGE quantum metric is achieved with a SMALL band gap → which forces SMALL |U| → "the superfluid weight remains small"** [VERIFIED, same]. The metric and the usable interaction TRADE OFF.
2. **Strong-coupling turnover:** T_BKT scales as |U| for |U|→0, **saturates at |U| ~ band gap, then DECREASES as U^−1** in the strong-coupling (bipolaron localization) limit [VERIFIED WebSearch, "BKT temperature saturates for an interaction of order the band gap and then decreases as U^−1"; cf. Huhtinen arXiv:2203.11133; synthesis paper route-3 "U-Ω dome, BCS-BEC localization caps Tc"].

So the geometric stiffness is NOT a free knob: it has a dome with a maximum near |U|~gap, and the maximum itself is held down because large ⟨g⟩ demands small gap (hence small usable U). The synthesis paper measured the net product deficit: **⟨g⟩·U is ~5× too small** in any realizable ambient host [VERIFIED, closed-negative paper abstract: "a ∼5× standing deficit in ⟨g⟩·U"]. This caps the geometric route back BELOW cuprate values — it does NOT lift it above. Counter-lens 3a CONFIRMS its own ceiling.

### 3b — Do enhanced low-carrier fluctuations LOWER the real Tc below the stiffness estimate? YES.

Flat-band SC has very low carrier number / small superfluid density → the phase is soft, fluctuations are strong, and the real Tc sits at or below the BKT/Uemura stiffness estimate, never above it [VERIFIED WebSearch, Emery-Kivelson Nature 374,434; Uemura scaling]. This is the SAME mechanism that caps cuprates, now operating on an even smaller stiffness — so it bites HARDER for flat bands, not softer. The measured MATBG/tTLG Tc ~1-3 K is already the fluctuation-limited (BKT-like, phase-controlled) value, not an over-estimate to be discounted further. Counter-lens 3b lowers (never raises) the geometric Tc.

### 3c — Is there a MEASURED flat-band SC whose geometric stiffness EXCEEDED the conventional ceiling? NO — the inverse is measured.

This is the decisive adversarial check, and it goes AGAINST escape:

- **MATBG:** superfluid stiffness measured ~10× above the *conventional Fermi-liquid* value (geometric contribution dominant, as Peotta-Törmä predicts) — yet Tc only **~1.0-1.7 K** [VERIFIED, Tian et al., Nature s41586-024-08494-7 / arXiv:2406.13740; max stiffness 1.7×10⁸ H⁻¹, ~order-of-magnitude above conventional]. The geometry enhancement is REAL and MEASURED, and it still leaves Tc ~90× below the cuprate ceiling.
- **twisted trilayer graphene:** Tc ~2-3 K, "phase-fluctuation-controlled" SC — geometric/nodal-gap, stiffness-limited [VERIFIED, Banerjee et al., Nature s41586-024-08444-3].
- **Re6Se8Cl2** (flat-band superatomic crystal, phonon-flattened bands): doping-induced SC Tc **~8 K** [VERIFIED, Nano Lett. 10.1021/acs.nanolett.9b04891 / arXiv:1906.10785].
- **CsV3Sb5 / kagome flat-band family:** Tc ~3-6 K, competing-order (CDW)-limited [VERIFIED, synthesis paper route-1; carried].

**No measured flat-band geometric superconductor reaches even 10 K.** The geometric stiffness, where directly measured, is enormous *relative to the vanishing band-velocity stiffness* but small in *absolute* terms — and it has NEVER exceeded, or even approached, the cuprate ~133 K conventional phase-stiffness ceiling. Counter-lens 3c is the strongest evidence that the ceiling is UNIVERSAL: the foundational mechanism's best real-world realizations land ~20-90× *below* the cuprate record, not above it.

## Synthesis — does the geometric stiffness ESCAPE the ceiling?

**No. The cuprate ~134 K phase-stiffness ceiling is UNIVERSAL, and the flat-band geometric route lands an order of magnitude WORSE.** Walking the lens:

- The geometric D_s is real, anomalous, topology-protected (≥|C|), and finite where the band velocity is zero — the campaign's foundational mechanism is sound (ED 70× check, route-0 green).
- BUT in *absolute* Kelvin-equivalent stiffness it is **~0.06-0.44 meV (Tc 1-8 K) — ~20-90× BELOW the cuprate ~7.4 meV (134 K) phase stiffness**, and ~700-5000× below the ~300 meV pairing scale.
- A flat-band geometric SC is therefore **STIFFNESS-limited** (Tc = min(pairing, stiffness) = stiffness), measured directly in MATBG and tTLG.
- Its stiffness has its OWN ceiling (U·g saturation: large metric ⇒ small gap ⇒ small usable U; T_BKT saturates at U~gap then falls as U^−1; synthesis-paper ⟨g⟩·U ~5× deficit), and low-carrier fluctuations bite harder, not softer.
- No measured flat-band geometric SC has ever exceeded — or neared — the cuprate ceiling.

So the dispatch's "escape branch" (D_s^geom ≥ pairing → pairing-limited → climb to the clean ~252 K coordinate) is **NOT realized**. The honest reading is the other branch: D_s^geom ≪ pairing → stiffness-limited → the cuprate-class phase-stiffness ceiling bites, and for the flat-band route it bites at an even *lower* value (~1-8 K measured) than for cuprates (~134 K). The cuprate ceiling is the BEST any mechanism has done at 1 atm, and geometric flat bands are the *lowest* rung on that ladder [VERIFIED, synthesis paper Fig./Table: geometric route sits lowest; cuprate Hg-1223 133 K is the unbeaten ambient record].
