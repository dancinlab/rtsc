### #1 — Stiffness Donor Co-Tenancy (Leggett multiband) [seed #70] + Velocity-Asymmetric Co-Drag [#73]
*Why top:* This is the single most *direct* in-silico attack on the frozen mechanism. The wall's derivation (`research-geometric-stiffness...`) explicitly states it was measured "on systems where ONE band does both jobs." Multiband co-tenancy is the one structural escape the freeze did **not** test: pairing on a flat/high-DOS band, stiffness *borrowed* from a co-resident deep dispersive band (D_s∝v_F²·N, eV-scale, no cuprate cap), phase-locked intrinsically by Leggett coupling — **no interlayer tunneling loss** (the killer that closed the multilayer-D_s lever). Settleable today with `flatchern_pair_ed.py`/`flatband_pair_ed.py` extended to 2 orbitals + `kubo_pair_stiffness.py`.

- **Frozen prediction:** In a 2-orbital BdG (flat band + dispersive band degenerate at E_F, interband attraction g₁₂), the composite Kubo D_s^tot = D_s^flat + D_s^deep, and T_BKT=(π/2)D_s^tot exceeds the flat-band-alone T_BKT by **>2×** when the deep band satisfies ½N_deep v_F² > (2/π)k_B·134 K.
- **Falsifiers:** (1) When g₁₂ is turned on, the Leggett mode goes soft (mass²→0) and D_s^tot collapses to min(band stiffnesses) instead of summing. (2) Increasing v_F monotonically *decreases* the interband matrix element (decoupling) so donated stiffness peaks and falls before the 293K-equivalent. (3) The composite Kubo D_s computed at finite T tracks the flat-band value, not the sum (no donation). (4) Reaching D_s^deep above the cuprate 7.4 meV scale requires v_F driving the deep band to depair (gap closes by kinetic energy) before stiffness arrives.

### #2 — AB-Cage Breather / Line-Graph Satellite Ladder [seeds #59, #61]
*Why:* The freeze's load-bearing claim is D_s ∝ |U|·⟨g⟩ with **|U| forced small because a large quantum metric needs a small band gap** (the U-saturation ceiling). AB-caging breaks exactly that coupling — caging is enforced by *flux (a phase)*, not by a small gap, so |U| stays O(eV) (gap = lattice hopping t) while a flux-detuning δ adds *conventional* velocity stiffness on top of the geometric piece. Line-graph #61 is the same physics via the *mandatory* dispersive partner band. Directly runs in `flatchern_pair_ed.py` (attractive-Hubbard on rhombic/checkerboard) + Kubo.

- **Frozen prediction:** In attractive-Hubbard ED on a rhombic AB-caging chain, D_s swept vs flux-detuning δ at FIXED |U| shows a maximum at a **nonzero δ\*** that exceeds the δ=0 (purely geometric, caged) D_s by >2×, because the U-saturation ceiling does not bind (|U| is gap-independent).
- **Falsifiers:** (1) D_s(δ) is monotone decreasing from δ=0 (flux buys no |U|-independent stiffness). (2) Detuning δ that adds velocity simultaneously closes the caging gap and reintroduces the |U|<gap constraint (collapses to the frozen ceiling). (3) The Kubo D_s at δ\* still sits ≥20× below the 7.4 meV cuprate scale (geometric+velocity sum is bounded). (4) For the line-graph case, the λ\*-hybridized D_s peak does NOT exceed the isolated-flat-band (λ=0) value by >2× (partner band donates nothing).

### #3 — ETA-LOCK Liouvillian Dark-State Pump [seed #9] (+ Dark-State Pair Pump #11)
*Why:* The most *orthogonal* genuine escape: η-pairing ODLRO is **distance-independent (zero coherence length)** — there is *no phase gradient and no stiffness to thermally unbind*, so the Emery–Kivelson quantity the wall bounds simply does not exist for this order. ### #4 — Tkachenko Ceiling-Breaker (z=2 Goldstone) [seed #64]
*Why:* The cleverest "attack the formula, not the number" seed. The entire 134–164 K figure is the saturation of a **linear-Goldstone** BKT/3D-XY estimate.
